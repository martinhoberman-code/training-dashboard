Pre-session warm-up (~8 minutes)

Built on the RAMP protocol (Raise → Activate/Mobilize → Potentiate) — the standard structure strength coaches use because it raises body temperature and heart rate before you stretch or load anything, which is both more effective and safer for your back than jumping straight into mobility work cold. Do this before every session, calibration phase or not.

Ramping up safely with back pain

Change one variable at a time — load, volume, or complexity, not all three. Concretely: start at roughly half the prescribed sets and a lower RPE (4–6) than the program calls for, favor bodyweight/band options over loaded ones in your first sessions back, and only add load or volume in small steps (~10% or less) once you've had two pain-free sessions in a row at the current level. Avoid loading the spine into flexion (rounded-back lifts, weighted sit-ups) — build stability with the Big 3 (bird dog, side plank, dead bug) instead. Deload every 3–5 weeks even if things feel good. Stop immediately on sharp, radiating, or worsening pain rather than pushing through — that's a signal to back off and check with a doctor or PT, not a target to grind past.
Sources: PureGym, Chelmsford Physio, BackFitPro (McGill), Foundational Rehab, TrainingPeaks, Pliability.
